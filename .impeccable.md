## Design Context

### Users
Vawn's creative team — a small, close circle producing music video content for a Brooklyn/Atlanta hip-hop artist. The primary user is the artist/creative director themselves, working in a focused creative session: uploading reference photos, pasting lyrics, and generating scene-by-scene image and video prompts for NB2, Kling, and Higgsfield. The tool is used on desktop in a production context — this is where the creative vision gets translated into actionable prompts.

### Brand Personality
**Premium, polished, confident.**

Voice: Authoritative but understated. The interface doesn't explain itself — it assumes you know what you're doing. No hand-holding, no marketing copy, no unnecessary labels. Quiet confidence.

Emotional goals: The user should feel like they're working in a professional creative suite purpose-built for their workflow — not a generic AI wrapper. It should feel like a creative instrument, not a form.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Dark, editorial, cinematic** — evolved toward a more premium, high-end creative tool feel.

- **Theme**: Dark mode only. Warm-tinted darks (current `#0c0b0a` base is good), not cold blue-blacks.
- **Color**: Gold (`#d4a03a`) as primary accent. Teal and violet as secondary platform indicators. Warm off-white text. No pure black, no pure white.
- **Typography**: Instrument Serif italic for display/branding. Raleway for UI. The pairing works — refine rather than replace.
- **References**: Runway/Pika-adjacent — sleek dark AI creative tools, but with more personality and warmth. No specific site to copy; the goal is to feel unique.
- **Anti-references**: Generic AI tools (cyan-on-dark, purple gradients, ChatGPT wrapper aesthetic), toy/playful apps (rounded pastels, emoji-heavy), corporate dashboards (charts, metrics, enterprise), cluttered/busy interfaces (feature overload, settings everywhere).
- **Film grain overlay**: Keep — it adds analog warmth and character.

### Design Principles

1. **Content is the hero** — The generated prompts and scene cards are the product. Everything else (header, controls, chrome) should recede. Maximize canvas, minimize UI furniture.

2. **Quiet luxury over loud effects** — Premium feel comes from spacing, typography, and restraint — not from gradients, glows, or glassmorphism. If it looks like AI made it, it's wrong.

3. **Built for flow state** — This is a creative production tool. Reduce friction between intent and output. The composer bar should feel like a creative instrument, not a form with fields.

4. **Warm, not cold** — Tint everything warm. The darks are chocolate/espresso, not slate/charcoal. The gold is honey, not neon yellow. Even the neutrals lean amber, not gray.

5. **Show confidence through restraint** — Fewer borders, fewer containers, fewer labels. Let spacing and hierarchy do the work. Trust the user to understand the interface.
