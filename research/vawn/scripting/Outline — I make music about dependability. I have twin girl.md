# Outline — I make music about dependability. I have twin girl

> [!info] SHORT format | Target: 45s | 4 sections

**Key Takeaway:** Dependability isn't a concept — it's a sound, and having twin daughters made Vawn hear it differently.
**CTA:** Drop a 🎯 if you've ever felt the weight of someone fully counting on you.

## Section 1 — The Hook — Name The Pressure (8s)

**Talking Points:**
- Open mid-sentence, no intro — 'I make music about dependability. Not loyalty. Not love. Dependability.'
- Let that word land with weight — slow it down slightly
- No smile, no hype — flat authority, let the stillness do the work

> [!example] Visual Aid
> Tight medium shot, direct eye contact to camera, minimal movement — artist seated or leaning, low-key environment (home studio, kitchen table, dim natural light)

**Source:** None — this is a cold open, raw presence only

*Transition: Cut hard — no pause — straight into the next line as if continuing a thought*

## Section 2 — The Reality Check — What It Actually Feels Like (20s)

**Talking Points:**
- My twin girls just turned one. Two of them. At the same time.
- There's no calling out. No off days. No 'I'll get to it tomorrow.'
- When I'm in the booth at 1am, it's not ambition — it's them. That's the pressure that's in the music.
- Describe the specific feeling: 'You either show up or you don't. They don't know the difference yet — but I do.'

> [!example] Visual Aid
> Quick b-roll cut: hands on studio boards or writing in a notebook — then cut back to face. Optional: a single still photo of the twins (no video of minors needed) held up briefly or shown on phone screen — kept tasteful and brief

**Source:** Personal lived experience — no outside references needed. Authenticity IS the source.

*Transition: Pause one beat, slight lean forward — signal a shift from emotion to craft*

## Section 3 — The Art Connection — How It Shapes The Sound (12s)

**Talking Points:**
- That pressure has a sound — heavy drums, low-end that sits in your chest
- It doesn't sound like struggle, it sounds like resolve
- Reference the album in progress without over-explaining: 'I've got 11 tracks. Every one of them knows what I'm carrying.'

> [!example] Visual Aid
> Cut to 5-8 seconds of audio-reactive waveform or brief studio session clip — headphones on, eyes closed, in the zone. Text overlay: 'Debut album in progress.' Simple, no frills.

**Source:** Pull a 5-second instrumental snippet from the album — something with heavy bass and deliberate rhythm that sonically represents the theme

*Transition: Music bed fades slightly under voice — bring up audio just enough to feel, then cut to final section*

## Section 4 — The Close — Leave Them With The Weight (5s)

**Talking Points:**
- One final line, unhurried: 'This is what dependability sounds like.'
- Do not explain further — let the silence after it breathe
- No outro music sting, no call to action spoken aloud — let the CTA live in the caption

> [!example] Visual Aid
> Static medium shot — hold for 2 full seconds after the line before cutting to black or freeze frame. Optional text card fade in: 'Vawn. Debut album coming.' — clean serif or minimal sans-serif font, no animation

**Source:** None — the close is earned silence

*Transition: Hard cut to black or freeze frame — end*
