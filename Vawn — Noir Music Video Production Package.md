### STEP 1 — FACE (Soul Cinema)

Paste this into Soul Cinema. Run 3–5 times until the bone structure, eyes, and presence feel right. Keep the generation where the eyes carry weight — not softness.

```
Lean athletic man in his early 30s, warm medium-brown complexion, close-cropped 
fade with sharp edges, short well-maintained beard. Jawline defined, face angular 
but not severe. Eyes dark and direct — controlled, not aggressive. 
No expression beyond composure. Front-facing, white studio background, 
soft directional key light from upper left, minimal fill, 
ultra-realistic, photographic, 50mm equivalent.
```

**What you're selecting for:**
- Eyes that carry weight without menace
- Jaw: defined, not dramatic
- Presence: still, not stiff

### STEP 2 — OUTFIT (Soul Cinema)

Run separately from the face. Every material detail matters here — this becomes permanent across 50+ shots.

```
Black heavyweight ribbed-knit turtleneck, medium weight, slight texture visible. 
Charcoal wide-leg trousers with a subtle chalk pinstripe, high waist, clean break 
at the ankle. Black leather Derby shoes, matte finish, minimal sole. 
A dark olive overcoat draped over the shoulders but not through the arms — 
collar up slightly, settling naturally. No jewelry except a single

soft cinematic lighting, full body, ultra-realistic.
```

**Wardrobe reasoning:** The coat-over-shoulders silhouette reads as someone who moved fast and didn't finish dressing — authority without effort. The pinstripe adds texture for noir lighting to catch without reading as "dressed up."

## DIRECTOR'S TREATMENT

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
DIRECTOR'S TREATMENT
"Already" — Vawn
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

THE CONCEPT
A man who made his decision before the video started. We arrive after
the resolution — when the dust has settled internally but hasn't settled
anywhere else. The city doesn't know yet. The video is the walk between
knowing and being known.

THE EMOTIONAL JOURNEY
We open on control — not performed, structural. A man in motion with no
urgency because he's already where he needs to be mentally. By the
midpoint, the environment closes in — rain, narrow streets, wet glass —
and for one shot we see the weight of it. Not fear. Weight. By the final
frame, he's back to himself. The city is still moving around him. He isn't
moving with it. He's moving through it.

VISUAL IDENTITY
• Color palette:
  — Near-black (the ground he stands on, where he came from)
  — Amber sodium (every streetlight — warmth that doesn't comfort)
  — Ice blue (glass, wet surfaces, reflected sky — what the world offers)
  — Deep burgundy (accent — the one neon sign, a bottle, a coat lining)
  — Warm bone (his skin in key light — the only soft element)

• Lighting:
  Hard top-key from practical sources only — streetlights, a bare bulb,
  neon from off-frame. No fill. Every face is half in shadow. 
  Cinematographer reference: Gordon Willis — light that withholds.
  The viewer earns every detail they see.

• Locations:
  — Rain-slicked street at 3am: a city without witnesses
  — Underground parking structure: concrete, long shadows, 
    fluorescent tubes with flicker — civilization stripped of warmth
  — Glass elevator interior: compression, ascent, the moment of 
    transition between floors is the transition between worlds
  — Rooftop ledge: not danger — perspective. Above the city 
    he just walked through.

• Wardrobe:
  Dark pinstripe, coat draped not worn. Every shot, the coat hangs
  differently — physics tell the story. Still air: coat falls straight.
  Wind: coat lifts. Rain: coat darkens at the shoulders. 
  The clothing is a weather instrument.

• Camera language:
  Locked-off observation. We never follow with urgency. The camera 
  was already there — he walks into frame and out of it. Slow pushes 
  for interiority. Never handheld. This is surveillance, not pursuit.

• Editing rhythm:
  Long takes. Cuts that breathe against the beat, not with it.
  Let silence do work. The viewer adjusts to his pace.

NARRATIVE STRUCTURE
  Intro:          Empty wet street — he enters frame. City establishes itself.
  Verse 1:        Walking sequence — underground structure. 
                  He passes through fluorescent shadow.
  Pre-chorus:     Elevator. Close on his reflection in the glass door. 
                  Weight appears.
  ★ Chorus:       Rain-slicked street, wide — him at the center, 
                  city blurring past. The hero frame.
  Verse 2:        Rooftop. Different air. Different posture.
  ★ Chorus 2:     Return to street — same shot, different energy. 
                  Something resolved.
  Outro:          He walks out of frame. The city stays. The camera holds.

HERO SHOTS
  1. Rain-slicked 4-way intersection at 3am, amber sodium overhead, 
     Vawn at dead center, back to camera, coat lifting slightly in wind, 
     city lights smearing on wet asphalt in every direction. 
     The city is a painting. He is the only real thing in it.

  2. Underground parking structure, fluorescent tube at end of frame flickering, 
     Vawn in side profile walking toward it, one half of his face lit warm, 
     one half pure shadow, footsteps echoing visibly in the still air.

  3. Glass elevator, interior — his reflection stacked into infinite recursion 
     in the parallel glass panels, face angled down and away, 
     coat settling around his shoulders as the elevator rises.
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```
