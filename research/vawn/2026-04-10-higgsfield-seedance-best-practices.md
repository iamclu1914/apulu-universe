## Best Practices for Music Video Production

### 1. Character Development (Vawn)

**SoulCast 3.0 Protocol**:
- **Genre**: Drama/Urban (matches hip-hop aesthetic)
- **Budget**: $50M+ (ensures premium visual quality)
- **Era**: 2020s (contemporary authenticity)
- **Archetype**: Hero/Outcast (aligns with Vawn's Brooklyn-Atlanta narrative)
- **Build**: Athletic (matches artist physicality)
- **Outfit Strategy**: Multiple saved configurations for different track moods

**Character Lock Implementation**:
- Generate comprehensive character sheet (front/side/back)
- Purchase exclusive rights to prevent platform use by others
- Save multiple outfit variations for different song themes
- Tag consistently with `@Vawn` in all prompts

### 2. Camera-Led Direction

**Essential Movements for Hip-Hop**:
- **Following Shot**: Camera trails behind, captures journey narrative
- **Side Tracking**: Maintains energy while showing profile
- **Orbit**: Dynamic movement around artist, shows confidence
- **Push In**: Builds intensity for emotional moments
- **Pull Back**: Reveals environment, establishes context

**Avoid**:
- Front-facing confrontational shots (creates staring problem)
- Static cameras (reduces energy)
- Head-on framing (feels like photo portrait, not cinema)

### 3. Acquisition Context for Authenticity

**Camera Bodies by Mood**:
- **ARRI ALEXA 35**: Premium tracks, mainstream appeal
- **Sony VENICE**: Underground aesthetic, low-light scenes
- **Arriflex 16SR**: Raw documentary feel, street credibility

**Lens Selection**:
- **24mm**: Environmental storytelling, urban landscapes
- **50mm**: Intimate moments, emotional connection
- **14mm**: Spatial immersion, crowd scenes

**Lighting Philosophy**:
- Always specify physical light sources
- Urban environments: sodium vapor, neon, car headlights
- Studio setups: named fixtures, motivated shadows
- Natural light: specific time of day, weather conditions

### 4. Multi-Shot Sequence Structure

**Hip-Hop Video Template**:
```
Shot 1: Establishing (environment, sets location context)
Shot 2: Artist Introduction (medium shot, shows presence)
Shot 3: Lyrical Focus (close-up during key bars)
Shot 4: Movement/Energy (tracking shot, builds momentum)
Shot 5: Environmental Interaction (wide shot, shows relationship to space)
```

**Emotional Arc Integration**:
- **Hope**: Forward-looking moments, future visualization
- **Trust**: Confident stance, direct but not confrontational gaze
- **Anger**: Tension moments, aggressive movement
- **Joy**: Relief sequences, celebration

### 5. Audio Synchronization Strategy

**With Existing Tracks**:
- Upload mastered audio from Freq/Slate pipeline
- Use Seedance 2.0 for perfect beat alignment
- Mute generated audio to preserve our professional mix
- Generate multiple takes for different visual angles

**Native Generation**:
- Test Seedance 2.0 native audio for reference tracks
- Compare quality to Suno generation workflow
- Use for rough cuts and concept visualization
- Final audio always from Apulu pipeline
