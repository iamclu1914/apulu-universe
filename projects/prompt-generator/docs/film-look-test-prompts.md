# Film Look Test Prompts — NB2 Side-by-Side

Six prompts for testing retro/vintage photographic treatments. Copy each one as a single block into NB2. Same subject (Vawn), each tuned to the film's native scene type.

---

## 1. KODAK PORTRA 400 — WARM EDITORIAL

```
Vawn leans against the side of a 3-story brick walkup in Clinton Hill, late afternoon golden hour, one hand in his pocket, the other holding a half-lit cigarette, gaze drifting past the lens. Shot on Kodak Portra 400 35mm film, scanned at high resolution, vintage Canon 50mm f/1.4 prime lens wide open, shallow depth of field, creamy falloff behind him. Golden sunlight raking across the brick, skin tones rendered warm and soft with slightly muted reds, lifted black point, gentle halation on highlights, visible organic silver-halide grain, subtle magenta cast in the shadows. Natural light only, no fill. Slightly soft focus, no digital sharpening, Portra's signature creamy skin rendering, mild chromatic aberration at frame edges. Medium-format fashion editorial sensibility. 35mm aspect ratio.
```

---

## 2. CINESTILL 800T — HALATED NIGHT

```
Vawn standing under a buzzing yellow bodega sign at 2am, Atlanta side street, hands in the pockets of a loose overcoat, breath visible, head turned slightly toward the street. Shot on Cinestill 800T 35mm tungsten film, pushed one stop, Leica 35mm f/2 Summicron. Tungsten-balanced color — neon signage bleeds into heavy red halation rings around every light source, classic Cinestill glow. Deep cyan-leaning shadows, magenta cast in the mid-tones, visible coarse film grain. Wet pavement catching reflections of neon. No artificial fill light, available-light only. Cinematic low-light look, slight underexposure, lifted blacks, warm highlights blooming softly. 35mm aspect ratio, scanned with natural film density.
```

---

## 3. KODACHROME 64 — SATURATED DAYLIGHT

```
Vawn sitting on the hood of a parked 80s sedan in a sunlit empty lot, Brooklyn, midday, aviators on, arms crossed loose, faint smirk. Shot on Kodachrome 64 slide film, Nikon F3 with 50mm f/1.8 Nikkor, bright direct sunlight. Kodachrome's signature color response — deep saturated reds and warm yellows, crisp cyan sky, hard-edged shadows with defined contrast, mid-range punch, subtle green shift in foliage. Fine tight grain, almost imperceptible at this stock speed. Color slide film rendering: rich, punchy, slightly cool in the shadows but warm in the highlights. No digital look whatsoever, no HDR, no shadow lift — true transparency contrast. 3:2 aspect ratio, scanned from a mounted slide.
```

---

## 4. 90s DISPOSABLE CAMERA — FLASH CASUAL

```
Vawn in a fluorescent-lit laundromat at night, leaning back against a washing machine, bag of clothes at his feet, caught mid-laugh looking slightly off-frame. Shot on a 90s single-use disposable camera — Kodak Funsaver — direct on-camera flash, 32mm fixed plastic lens f/11, ISO 400 consumer color negative film. Hard flash shadow behind him on the wall, blown-out highlights on skin, slightly overexposed background ambient fluorescent bleeding through. Flat consumer-film color rendering, slight magenta cast, slightly soft plastic-lens focus, fingerprint smudge vignette in one corner. Orange date-stamp in the lower-right reading "10 28 97". Visible grain, cheap-film tonality. 3:2 aspect ratio, amateur casual snapshot feel, not posed.
```

---

## 5. SUPER 8 CINEMA STILL — GRAIN HEAVY MOTION

```
Vawn walking away from the camera down a narrow Atlanta alley at dusk, orange streetlamp kicking in, back turned, half-silhouette, one hand trailing along a chain-link fence. Shot on Super 8mm Kodak Vision3 200T color negative film, telecined to digital frame-by-frame, captured as single cinema still. Heavy visible organic grain structure, slight gate weave, color bleed in the highlights, soft edges from the small frame. Warm orange streetlamp reflecting off brick, deep teal shadows, 70s cinema color grade. Mild motion blur in his hand. Vintage C-mount 12mm prime lens, slight barrel distortion. 4:3 aspect ratio, cinema frame crop, no digital sharpening, telecine scan with authentic film density.
```

## 6. CONTAX T2 — OVEREXPOSED FASHION

```
Vawn sitting on a concrete stoop in Bed-Stuy, mid-morning, plain white tee and loose trousers, legs extended, looking directly into the lens with quiet confidence. Shot on Contax T2 compact camera, Carl Zeiss Sonnar 38mm f/2.8 T*, loaded with Fuji Pro 400H, deliberately overexposed by one stop for the Hedi Slimane casting-tape look. Blown-out highlights on skin and whites, cool green-cyan shadow bias, creamy desaturated mid-tones, high key with lifted exposure, very fine grain. On-camera pop-up flash used as mild fill against natural morning light, slight flash reflection in the shadows behind him. Minimalist fashion editorial feel, almost snapshot-casual but deliberately composed. Fuji's cool pastel skin rendering. 3:2 aspect ratio.
```

---

## Test protocol

Generate each at identical output size. Judge on:
1. Does it look like that film / camera? (fidelity)
2. Does it fit Vawn's brand world? (cream/gold/warm-darks — likely eliminates #3 and #6)
3. Could it survive 8 scenes in a row without getting monotonous? (sustainability)

Report back which 2-3 win and they get ported into `cinematographer.js` as locked presets.
